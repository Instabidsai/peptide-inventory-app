TB-500 is found in high concentrations in blood platelets and wound fluid, playing a critical role in healing and tissue repair.

The fundamental mechanism of action of TB-500 involves binding to actin, a structural protein in every cell. By regulating actin, TB-500 influences cell migration, proliferation, and differentiation. It acts as a signaling molecule that directs cells to their appropriate locations, facilitating the healing process.

When tissue is damaged, TB-500 is released immediately, promoting cell migration and guiding repair cells to the site of injury. It upregulates proteins like integrins, which are crucial for cell movement and repair. TB-500 also has anti-inflammatory properties, downregulating key inflammatory cytokines such as NF-kappa B, TNF-alpha, IL-1 beta, and IL-6. This action addresses the root cause of cellular damage rather than merely masking pain.

## Angiogenesis and Cancer Concerns

A common misconception is that TB-500 may cause or fuel cancer due to its promotion of angiogenesis, the formation of new blood vessels. While tumors require new blood vessels to grow, angiogenesis is a fundamental and tightly regulated process essential for healing. For example, when a wound occurs, angiogenesis is necessary to deliver immune cells and nutrients for repair.

To suggest that we should avoid angiogenesis because tumors might hijack it is a misunderstanding of human biology. Angiogenesis is not a rogue process; it is vital for recovery and growth. The fear surrounding TB-500 and cancer is based on a failure to understand the biological mechanisms at play.

Additionally, TB-500 reduces fibrosis by blocking the transformation of fibroblasts into myofibroblasts, the cells responsible for scar tissue formation. This ensures that healing occurs with functional tissue rather than just scar tissue.

## Conclusion

The notion that TB-500 could cause cancer is statistically unfounded. To cause cancer, three biological failures must occur: systemic inflammation, insulin resistance, and ATP shortage. TB-500 does not contribute to these conditions; instead, it supports the body's natural healing processes. Understanding the science behind TB-500 can empower individuals to make informed decisions about their health and treatment options.
