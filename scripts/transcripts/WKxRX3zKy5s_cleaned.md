## Understanding Cognitive Decline

Cognitive decline is primarily driven by mitochondrial dysfunction, neuroinflammatory problems, and cholinergic deficiencies. The brain, while only 2% of body weight, consumes 20-25% of the body's energy. When mitochondrial function deteriorates, the brain experiences symptoms like brain fog and slow processing speed. 

Neuroinflammation is triggered by poor diet, stress, and toxins, leading to oxidative stress that hampers cognitive function. Additionally, a deficiency in acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter crucial for learning and memory, contributes to cognitive decline.
