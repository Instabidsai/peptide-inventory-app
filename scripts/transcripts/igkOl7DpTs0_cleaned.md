# Mitochondrial Dysfunction and Metabolic Health

Mitochondrial dysfunction is a significant factor in metabolic diseases. This gradient is utilized by ATP synthase to produce ATP (adenosine triphosphate), the energy currency of the body. When this system breaks down due to oxidative stress, inflammatory damage, nutrient deficiencies, chronic caloric excess, or aging, mitochondrial dysfunction occurs, leading to inefficient ATP production.

A study published in 2023 showed that individuals with severe mitochondrial dysfunction, as indicated by decreased ATP production and increased reactive oxygen species, exhibited only 60% of the expected response to GLP-1 receptor agonists compared to those with healthy mitochondrial function.
