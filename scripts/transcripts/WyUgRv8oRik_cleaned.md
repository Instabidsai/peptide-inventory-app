The simultaneous activation of these receptors creates a unique metabolic response that is not achievable through single receptor agonists.

### GLP-1

GLP-1 is released from the gut when food is consumed. It signals the pancreas to release insulin and informs the brain that satiety has been reached. Single agonists like semaglutide mimic this action but can lead to various side effects and complications.

### GIP

GIP, released from the upper intestine, also stimulates insulin secretion. When combined with GLP-1, GIP enhances insulin sensitivity in adipose tissue, making fat cells more metabolically flexible and efficient.

### Glucagon

Glucagon is typically known as the fasting hormone, signaling the liver to release glucose. However, when combined with GLP-1 and GIP, glucagon's role shifts. Instead of raising blood sugar levels, it contributes to thermogenesis, effectively utilizing energy rather than allowing it to flood the bloodstream.
